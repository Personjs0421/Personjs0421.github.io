About Me

Hi, my name is Julian Soong and I go by personjs online. I have played under Intel Chips and carbonara, my Capture-The-Flag (CTF) teams. In my free time, I enjoy playing video games, cubing, and of course, coding and CTFs. I have been programming since I was in primary school and have been playing in CTFs since 2024.

My programming journey has been very enjoyable so far, going from thinking that "high-level language" meant "difficult to learn" to earning myself an olympiad medal. My cybersecurity journey has been similar, from trying to open an ELF file on a Windows machine... in notepad... to being one of the top CTF players in the country in my age group.

Achievements

YCEP/CCTF 2024

Unexpected but welcome surprise

After a shaky performance in the YCEP bootcamp and barely making the cut for the Central Capture The Flag (CCTF) competition, I managed to achieve my highest placing at the time, 4th place individual at the CCTF 2024. Achieving 4th place only 6 months after learning about CTFs showed me my potential and pushed my on my journey to become better.

BlahajCTF 2024

First time qualifying for physical finals of an online contest

While I technically participated in a team of 4, due to their circumstances the other 3 were only able to score around 2% of our total points, so I hacked away for almost a day straight and managed to qualify for the finals as the only secondary school team (to my knowledge). It was definitely a challenge playing essentially solo as a secondary schooler against teams of JC and polytechnic students, but it was a fun experience nonetheless (and I'm happy I even qualified honestly).

YBNCTF 2024

Just barely qualified

Similar to that year's BlahajCTF, I technically participated in a team of 3, but the other two were not able to help out in the qualifiers or finals. In the end, I went to the physical finals alone and managed to get 10th place, which was an improvement over my 11th place in the qualifiers.

YCEP 2025

My first win!

After a relatively disappointing performance in the 2024 YCEP, I came back in 2025 with my most dominant performance yet and first win in the SP YCEP bootcamp

NOI 2025

My introduction to Competitive Programming

Initially, I didn't really want to compete in the National Olympiad of Informatics (NOI), but after some contemplation I figured it would be worth a try. After my end-of-year examinations in 2024, I dedicated my time to learning C++, which was a new(ish) language for me, as well tons of algorithms and problem-solving skills. In the end, I managed to claim a satisfactory silver medal after 3-4 months of preparation. I'm aiming for a gold medal in 2026!

CCTF 2025

Not bad, but room for improvement

Following my 4th place placing in 2024, I wanted to do better in 2025. Although I was gunning for 1st but placed 3rd, I did manage to place higher than the last year, so I suppose all is well.

NCO 2025

My first olympiad gold!

I was pretty nervous and not very confident going into the National Cybersecurity Olympiad (NCO), competing against JC students with more experience, especially since it was in an individual competition. However, I somehow managed to pull through with a gold medal and 6th place overall, allowing me to compete in the inaugural International Cybersecurity Olympiad in late June.
